Te Whare Tapa Whā

  • What is Te Whare Tapa Whā

    Te Whare Tapa Whā is an holistic model of health that describes health as a wharenui/meeting house with four walls. These walls represent taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing), taha hinengaro (mental and emotional wellbeing), taha tinana (physical wellbeing) and taha whānau (family and social wellbeing).

  • Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

    After recovering from a chronic injury, I started to get back to play football again. But being unfit, I go to the gym at least 3 times a week and run 3 times a week early in the morning.

  • Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

    Technology makes contacting friends and family easier, but I prefer face-to-face interactions. My parents live in Auckland, and we pay a visit once a week, and daily video calls to check on them.

  • Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

    Recently, I started to meditate, and it helps me not to go 500 miles an hour, take things at a time and be more focused. I intend to do that daily before going to bed and practice breathing to relax my head before bed.

  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

    I pray daily in the morning when walking my pooch.

  • Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

    I enjoy walking barefooted since moved to New Zealand and when the weather allows, I do that.
