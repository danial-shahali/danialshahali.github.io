Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

  • Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.
    Growing up in different countries has its goods and the downside side of things. The good thing is that you learn how to respect diversity and appreciate other cultures. That improved my lifestyle and allowed me to learn how to be adaptable. The downside of it would be having no long-term friends and keep making a new one until you are afraid to look for one… I had a choice to be an international student or a foreigner to work there, but that limited me in different aspects. For instance, when I moved to China, I had a tough time doing a simple task at work, as I had no help from a local translator! At first, I tried to change my colleagues’ working mentality that was a huge failure. So I had to make an ethical decision to change my way of work and learn their culture, their language. I put in extra effort after an hour, which led my colleague to approach and help me to learn better and help me to do my work. I kept that attitude of adaptability and built on it wherever I went, and that helped me to grow as a person and a professional.
  • Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.
    I grew up in a diverse society for my entire life, People around me had a great influence on the person I am today, and I learned to appreciate life no matter what and be responsible for it even if I am in an awkward situation that I did not influence in at all, but this is my life and my responsibility to take action regardless. And that is one of the main reasons I am here at Dev Academy
  • Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.
    Determination is one of my strengths in life, and that never let me down. I had to learn different languages to communicate with people and learn new cultures to live with different people, which I relied on in my studies to be a software developer. It is not entirely easy to stop what you do and start something new, in my case, I am in technology, and I do like my job very much, but being a developer is what I want to be so that I can reach my long term career goal, Hence, the desire of being one, helped me to make this decision to pause life for a bit and take on this course.
  • Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?
    My greatest limitation is my desire to know everything and be good at my work. Technology is a fast-paced and broad industry, and that is where I get frustrated and impacts my work. I might get to the stage where there is much to learn, and I have not accomplished what I planned for, and that is when things start to slow down, and I might need assistance.
  • Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.
    I had a colleague who was quite knowledgeable but needed more experience. I had a challenge working with him as my colleague was not keen to change and formulate his working ethic that suited the team best. So, there needed to be more transparent communication on what was done and what was not, and that impacted the work delivery. I was diminished due to my lack of technical education back then by him and was called stupid several times. I was even accused of throwing hurdles and not letting my colleague do the work. The situation escalated, and we were called to HR. Then, we were assigned a mutual buddy to help us communicate, as I understand the business processes better, and he has the technical understanding, and we must merge to succeed. The middle person has formed our way of communication, and that lets us start talking directly without fighting. We improved our relationship and understood that my colleague thought I was getting credit for the work. At the same time, I intended to stand up for him and speak out for the job that was done, where we could have communicated more about it. I have learned a valuable lesson: if there is a conflict, attack the argument, not the person, and have a simple conversation to understand the situation before jumping to a conclusion and act.
